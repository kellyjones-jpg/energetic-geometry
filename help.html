PV Technology — Light Distortion Patterns

PV Technology Warp Style Visual Strategy Conceptual Rationale
Monofacial PV Linear Warp Shapes bend one way; converging lines; stable downward tilt Mimics single-direction light collection with lens-like planar flow
Bifacial PV Symmetric Warp / Mirror Bilateral waveforms; bulging vertical shapes; moiré overlap effects Reflects dual light intake; symmetry suggests front and rear absorption
Translucent PV Radial / Organic Warp Rippled, circular distortions; transparent mesh with center deformation Suggests permeability, diffusion, greenhouse light conditions

Crop Type — Edge Style / Curvature

Crop Group Edge Style / Curvature Visual Metaphor / Rationale
Root vegetables Pointed/tapered edges Represents downward growth and defined root tips
Leafy greens & herbs Wavy/frilled edges Mimics soft, rippling leaf textures
Fruit-bearing crops Bulbous/lobed outlines Reflects swollen, multi-lobed fruit shapes
Berry/small fruits Concentric dot rings Clusters of small modular fruit bodies
Grains & grasses Straight, fine linear spikes Upright narrow growth of grains
Vining/perennials Spiraled/looping overlays Twisting vines and interwoven growth
Mixed/assorted crops Layered/composite edges Combines multiple edge patterns for diversity

Treatment for Multiple Crop Types:

  • Morphological blending using Bézier curves to create compound outlines.
  • Edge complexity increases with diversity (1–2 crops simple; 6+ irregular, organic forms).
  • Edge wave frequency/amplitude modulated by crop count and type contribution.
